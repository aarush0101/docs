Becoming a Trainee at NetherGames involves several stages: submitting an application, passing an internal review, completing an interview, and then going through a structured training programme. This article walks you through each stage so you know what to expect.

## Eligibility requirements

Before you can submit a Trainee application, you must meet all of the following conditions:

- Your Discord account is connected to your NetherGames account
- You have been a member of the NetherGames Discord server for at least 30 days
- You joined the NetherGames Minecraft network more than 30 days ago
- You have no pending Trainee applications
- You have no active punishments on your account
- You are not currently a Trainee

## The application process

<Steps>
  <Step title="Submit your application">
    Go to [ngmc.co/request](https://ngmc.co/request) and sign in to your NetherGames Portal account. Select the **Trainee** application type and press **Continue** once your eligibility is confirmed.

    When filling out the form, provide as much detail as possible and answer honestly. The review team evaluates your skills, attitude, grammar, ability to detect cheaters, and your activity on the network.

    Press **Submit** when you are done. If you do not receive a response within 7–10 days, reach out to the team via **#support** on the NetherGames Discord server.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Internal review">
    A supervisor reviews your application first. If they feel you show potential, they begin a background check. Factors considered during this stage include:

    - Behaviour on the network and on the Discord server
    - Both expired and active punishments
    - Your written responses to application questions and scenarios

    If your background check comes back clear, your application status changes to **Escalated** and moves to an Admin of People & Culture for final review. If the Admin approves, your status changes to **Approved** and you will be invited to an interview.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Interview">
    Once your application is approved, you will receive a Discord role and be assigned to a dedicated interview channel. Here you will arrange the best time for your interview and can ask any questions you have.

    You may choose to conduct your interview via text or voice — however, **all interviews must be conducted in English**. During voice interviews, the interviewer may take notes throughout the session.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Final review">
    After the interview, an Admin of People & Culture reviews both your interview and your application form together. This is typically a final look-over before the decision is made.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Acceptance">
    If you are accepted, you will receive an email with the next steps. Two important actions are required before your Trainee tag is assigned:

    1. **Sign the NetherGames Non-Disclosure Agreement.** This agreement ensures all staff respect the privacy of the team and keep distributed information confidential.
    2. **Provide an identification document** to confirm your identity. You may censor addresses or photos, but your full name and date of birth must be clearly visible and the document must be shown in full to confirm its validity.

    Send both documents as email attachments to the NetherGames People & Culture department. Once received, an Admin will assign your Trainee roles across the network and announce your arrival in the Staff Announcements channel on Discord.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What you will do as a Trainee

As a Trainee, you will be trained by the mentor team on moderation practices, punishment types (both in-game and on Discord), and how to handle special situations. You will gain access to additional in-game commands, but you should refer to the Staff Handbook and your mentor's guidance before using any moderation tools.

<Note>
  During training, Trainees do not issue punishments directly. Instead, you will report players through an internal system used to track each Trainee's progress. Evidence is required for all reports regardless of your role — even after promotion to Crew.
</Note>

After a few weeks of training, the Supervisor and Admin team will review your progress and decide whether you are ready to be promoted to Crew. Crew members gain full ban and mute permissions across the network and no longer follow the stricter Trainee punishment guidelines.

If you have questions not covered here, send a message to our Modmail bot on [Discord](https://ngmc.co/d).
