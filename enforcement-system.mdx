title: "How the NetherGames Enforcement System Works"
description: "How the points-based punishment system works, every offence category and its point value, and how punishments apply to linked accounts."
---

NetherGames uses a points-based enforcement system. When a staff member issues a punishment, points are added to your account. Once your points reach a threshold in a given category, a ban or mute is triggered for a set duration. Points are tracked per category — so points from one type of offence do not combine with points from another.

## Point thresholds

Accumulating points in a category triggers the following punishments:

| Points | Duration |
|--------|----------|
| 2 | 1 day |
| 4 | 1 week |
| 6 | 2 weeks |
| 8 | 1 month |
| 10 | 2 months |
| 12 | 4 months |
| 14 | 8 months |
| 16 | Indefinite |

<Note>
  Thresholds are tracked **per category**. For example, if you have a punishment for cheating and a separate one for teaming, they do not combine. You would receive two separate punishments, one per category.
</Note>

## How points reduce over time

Points are not permanent. If you go one full month without receiving a punishment, your account's total points are reduced by one. This means you can return your account to good standing through continued good behaviour.

<Note>
  While points reduce over time, every punishment issued is permanently added to your account's record and cannot be removed. Your history is used for administrative purposes only — for example, when you apply for a staff position. Only the points total is used to determine punishment duration.
</Note>

## How punishments apply to linked accounts

All punishments apply to the account that received them **and** any other accounts associated with the same device or player.

For example:
- If you share your device with a sibling and they receive a ban, that ban also applies to your account.
- If you previously gave your login details to another player and they were banned while using your account, that punishment will apply retroactively to you — even if you were not the one who broke the rules.

## Evidence

All punishments issued by staff are supported by evidence collected at the time of the offence. That evidence is reviewed during the appeals process by a member of the Appeals & Disputes Division — a different person from the staff volunteer who issued the punishment.

Evidence is for internal use only and will not be shared with players or third parties. You will not be able to review the evidence submitted against you.

If you believe a punishment was issued in error, you can submit an appeal at [ngmc.co/request](https://ngmc.co/request).
