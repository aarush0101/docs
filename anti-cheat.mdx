title: "NetherGames Anti-Cheat: How It Works for Players"
description: "What the NetherGames anti-cheat system detects, why it might kick you even if you're not cheating, and what to do if you think a ban was issued unfairly."
---

NetherGames runs an automated anti-cheat system that monitors gameplay in real time to detect rule-breaking behaviour. Understanding how it works can help you avoid unexpected kicks and know what to do if something goes wrong.

## Current status

The anti-cheat system is currently in **beta**. This means false-positive kicks — where you are removed from a game even though you weren't cheating — are possible. We apologise for any inconvenience this causes and are actively working to improve its accuracy.

<Note>
  The anti-cheat system **never issues bans directly**. It can kick you from a game, but any ban requires a staff member to manually review evidence first. No automated ban will ever be issued without human review.
</Note>

## What to do if you're kicked unexpectedly

If the anti-cheat kicks you and you believe it was a mistake:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Rejoin the server">
    A kick is not a ban. You can reconnect to NetherGames immediately after being kicked.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Report the false positive">
    Head to **#bugs** in our [Discord server](https://ngmc.co/discord) and report the issue. Include as much detail as possible — what you were doing, what game mode you were playing, and any client or resource packs you were using.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What to do if you receive a ban

Since the anti-cheat never issues bans on its own, any ban you receive has been reviewed and issued by a staff member. If you believe your ban was unjustified:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Submit an appeal">
    Go to [ngmc.co/request](https://ngmc.co/request) and open an appeal. Explain why you believe the punishment was issued in error.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Wait for the review">
    Your appeal will be reviewed by a member of the Appeals & Disputes Division — a different person from the staff volunteer who issued the punishment.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Do not attempt to evade your ban by joining on an alternate account while your appeal is pending. Ban evasion carries serious consequences under our [Enforcement System](/enforcement-system).
</Warning>
