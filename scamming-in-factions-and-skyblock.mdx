---
title: "Scamming Rules in Factions and Skyblock"
description: "Understand what scamming is and isn't punishable in Factions and Skyblock, and how to protect yourself when trading with other players."
---

Scamming is not punishable in Factions or Skyblock. This means that if another player scams you out of items or currency, the NetherGames team cannot compensate you for what you lost. You are responsible for the trades and purchases you make, and you should approach all player-to-player transactions with caution.

<Warning>
  Because scamming is not punishable, the team cannot restore items or currency lost in a scam. There are no exceptions to this policy for standard item trading in Factions or Skyblock.
</Warning>

You are also responsible for every setting and structure on your island. If another player steals items from your island or destroys your buildings, the team cannot assist you with recovering those losses.

<Tip>
  To reduce your risk of being scammed, always buy and sell items through the Auction House (`/ah`) or the trading system (`/trade`) rather than informal arrangements with other players.
</Tip>

## What is punishable

While scamming itself is allowed, **teleport trapping** is a punishable offence. Teleport trapping occurs when a player asks you to teleport to their location in Factions or to an island in Skyblock under the pretence of trading or purchasing items, and then kills you before or after the transaction. You can report teleport trapping with evidence at [ngmc.co/request](https://ngmc.co/request).

## Trading real-world items or currency

<Warning>
  Even though scamming is permitted in Factions and Skyblock, trading real-world items — including ranks or real money — in exchange for in-game items is never allowed. This applies even if the transaction turns out to be a scam. Any involvement in IRL money trading, including simply suggesting it, is punishable under the **IRL Money Trading** rule regardless of the outcome.
</Warning>

<Note>
  The IRL Money Trading rule applies whether or not the transaction is completed. Simply proposing to trade a rank or real currency for in-game items is enough to receive a punishment.
</Note>
