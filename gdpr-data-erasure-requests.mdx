---
title: "GDPR Data Erasure Requests for NetherGames"
description: "EU players can request full deletion of their NetherGames account data under GDPR, including a downloadable data export before erasure is processed."
---

If you are based in the European Union, you have the right to request the erasure of your personal data under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). NetherGames processes these requests through the support portal, and you can also request a full copy of your data before it is deleted.

## How to request data erasure

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to the support portal">
    Open [ngmc.co/request](https://ngmc.co/request) in your browser.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Sign in with your Xbox account">
    Log in with the Microsoft (Xbox) account linked to your NetherGames profile.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Account Deletion">
    Choose **Account Deletion** from the request type list and submit the form.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Request a data export (optional)">
    Per EU regulation, you can also request a full copy of the data being erased in either JSON or CSV format. To do this, contact the team via Modmail in the [NetherGames Discord server](https://discord.gg/UFQfD6ThVF).
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
NetherGames reserves the right to remove internal company data from exported data batches where that data is not directly linked to your account.
</Note>

## What gets deleted

A data erasure request removes the following data associated with your account:

- **Player profile:** Ranks and perks, purchases, XP, level, tier, and achievements
- **Social profile:** Guild membership, friend list, and party status
- **Statistics profile:** Statistics for all games
- **Record profile:** Chat logs and match replay participation

<Warning>
Punishments are not deleted as part of a GDPR erasure request. Because punishments result from a breach of the Terms of Service, this data falls under the GDPR's "operational data" exclusion — NetherGames has a legitimate, reasonable interest in retaining it for community safety purposes. Punishment data may be kept for up to 12 months or until the punishment expires, whichever comes first.
</Warning>
